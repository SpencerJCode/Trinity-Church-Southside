Book of Confessions
Creeds and confessions express an important part of who we are as a church. We confess and believe together with our fathers in the faith and our brothers throughout the world (1 Cor 14:36; Gal 3:28; 1 Cor 3:1–4). We are baptized into their company as members of the same body, the Church of our Lord Jesus Christ, and with them we eat of a common loaf and drink from a common cup; their creed is our creed, even as their life is our life—one Lord, one faith, and one baptism (Eph 4:4–6). We therefore subscribe to the following, holding them to be a faithful witness to what the Scriptures teach, and as a means of identifying with the broader Church: the Apostles’ Creed, the Nicene Creed, the Definition of Chalcedon, the Athanasian Creed, and the Canons of Dort.
